GULAB JAMUN

Gulab Jamun is one of the most popular and loved sweets in India.
Its melt-in-the-mouth texture and rich flavor make it a favorite dessert for people of all ages.


Ingredients:

-1 cup khoya (or milk powder as substitute)
-3 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
-1 pinch baking soda
-2 tablespoons milk (for kneading)
-Ghee or oil (for frying)
-1½ cups sugar
-1½ cups water
-4–5 cardamom pods
-Few saffron strands or 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Method:

In a bowl, mix khoya, flour, and baking soda. Add milk little by little and knead into a smooth, soft dough.
Divide the dough into small equal portions and roll them into smooth balls without cracks.
Heat ghee/oil on low flame and fry the balls until golden brown. Make sure the flame is not too high, or the inside will remain uncooked.
In another pan, prepare sugar syrup by boiling water, sugar, and cardamom until slightly sticky. Add saffron or rose water for flavor.
Soak the fried balls in the warm sugar syrup for at least 1–2 hours.
Serve warm or at room temperature.


Cooking Terms:

Knead – Mix and press dough to make it soft.

Dough – Soft mixture of flour and liquid.

Fry – Cook in hot oil or ghee.

Syrup – Sweet liquid of sugar and water.

Soak – Keep in liquid to take in flavor.

✨ Now you have delicious Gulab Jamuns ready!
